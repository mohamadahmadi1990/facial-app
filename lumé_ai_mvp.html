Pillar 01 — Hardware

The LUMÉ Mask

A clinical-grade wearable designed for daily at-home use, powered by multi-modal therapy and real-time AI skin analysis.

Technical Specifications

  • Light Therapy 850nm Infrared · 630nm Red · 415nm Blue — targeting collagen, inflammation, and acne in distinct zones
  • Microcurrent Adjustable 0–300µA stimulation to lift, tone, and re-educate facial muscles with clinical precision
  • Sensors 4× embedded skin hydration, sebum, elasticity, and melanin sensors updating every 2 seconds
  • Thermal Zones Heated and cryo panels for enhanced product absorption and lymphatic stimulation
  • AI Engine On-device + cloud AI (OpenAI Vision API + GPT-4o) analyzes skin in real time and adjusts treatment parameters dynamically
  • Connectivity Bluetooth 5.3 to iOS & Android app · Wi-Fi OTA firmware updates
  • Battery 90-minute runtime · 45-min fast charge via USB-C
  • Materials Medical-grade silicone body · PMMA optical panels · Hypoallergenic contact surfaces

Competitive Advantage

Why LUMÉ wins

Full-Stack Integration

Competitors like CurrentBody or Foreo sell devices with no AI. Competitors like Curology do software with no hardware. Competitors like Face Gym do clinics with no tech. LUMÉ is the only brand operating all four layers simultaneously — creating compounding network effects and a data moat that is nearly impossible to replicate.

The Skin Data Flywheel

Every session generates anonymized skin response data. More data → better AI → better outcomes → more users → more data. After 18 months, this dataset becomes a defensible proprietary asset no competitor can buy or replicate.

High-LTV Bundle Lock-in

Hardware → app subscription → product replenishment → clinic bookings → consulting. A typical LUMÉ customer has 4+ revenue touchpoints. Average LTV is projected at $2,800 over 24 months vs industry average of $180 for single-product beauty brands.

AI-Personalization at Scale

No two LUMÉ users get the same experience. The AI continuously adapts treatment intensity, product recommendations, dietary advice, and clinic scheduling based on real-time biometric data — delivering clinical-grade personalization that was previously only available at $500/session appointments.
